Regional Overview

The Americas is the world's fastest-growing gambling region with 13-18% CAGR and the lowest average tax rates globally (8-15%). This combination creates exceptional opportunities for both entry and scaling. Brazil represents the most urgent priority as the region's largest and fastest-consolidating market, while Dominican Republic offers the fastest entry path globally (45 days). The region is characterized by rapid regulatory maturation and increasing consumer adoption of digital payment methods.

2026 Critical Opportunity: Brazil's regulated market opened January 1, 2025, creating a 12-18 month window for operators to establish market position before consolidation occurs (industry analysts predict 65 operators will compress to 30-40 by end 2026).

Status: REGULATED MARKET (OPENED JAN 1, 2025)
Regulator: SPA/MF (Secretariat for Prizes & Bets)
Tax Rate: 18% GGR

Entry Time: 60 days application + 150 days review
Entry Difficulty: High (due diligence intensive)

Market Dynamics & Opportunity:

  • Market Growth Trajectory: 36-39M active accounts by 2026 (9% of adult population); $10B by 2029
  • Current Status: 65 licensed operators running 171 brands as of August 2025
  • Market Share Available: Consolidation phase beginning; operators report licensing fees causing smaller players to exit or merge
  • Entry Window: 12-18 months before market stabilizes with 30-40 major operators

2026 Regulatory Developments:

  • Supplier Approval Roadmap: SPA/MF announcing new regulations governing platform providers, game studios, live casino vendors throughout 2025-2026
  • Goal: Prevent unauthorized content leakage to offshore markets
  • National Betting System (NBS): Expected 2025-2026 implementation to centralize bet monitoring and responsible gaming controls
  • AI Integration: Operators must implement AI-powered bet creation, player personalization, churn prediction, risk management
  • Payment Integration: PIX dominance; traditional debit cards; TED transfers; book transfers approved
  • Facial Recognition: Mandatory KYC requirement for player onboarding

Critical Success Factors for Brazil Entry:

  • Platform Selection: Choose providers with documented compliance frameworks and SPA/MF recognition
  • RNG Certification: All games must pass statistical randomness tests; certified RNG infrastructure non-negotiable
  • Local Presence: Brazilian headquarters required (not optional); local bank account mandatory
  • Sports Integrity: Membership in sports integrity monitoring organizations required
  • Capital Requirements: Minimum working capital thresholds increasing; adequate reserves critical for sustainability

Strategic Urgency: THIS IS THE #1 PRIORITY FOR AMERICAS EXPANSION. The regulatory window for new operator entry is open NOW but closing. Within 12-18 months, market will consolidate and barriers to entry will increase substantially. Competitive positioning requires immediate action.

  • Digital-First Regulation: All new Americas markets prioritizing digital/iGaming over land-based, reversing traditional regulatory sequence
