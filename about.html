About This Guide

This site condenses a 10,000+ line analytical dataset covering 75+ online casino and sports betting markets into an actionable market‑entry and portfolio‑management tool for 2025–2026.[file:1]

Scope & Coverage

The guide tracks more than 75 markets across the Americas, Europe, Asia, and Africa, with a combined 2026 online market size of USD 51.9B–61.65B and an overall growth outlook of 10–15% CAGR.[file:1]

  • 13 markets in the Americas, including Tier‑1 Latin American jurisdictions.[file:1]
  • 30+ markets in Europe, covering low‑tax gateways, standard EU regimes, and closed monopolies.[file:1]
  • 18 markets in Asia, of which only a small subset are practically addressable for compliant operators.[file:1]
  • 10 markets in Africa, focused on early‑stage, high first‑mover advantage opportunities.[file:1]

Intended Audience

The content is designed for operators, investors, and internal strategy, finance, and compliance teams planning online gambling expansion from Q1 2026 onward.[file:1]

  • Operators evaluating new licences, white‑label/B2B structures, or M&A targets.
  • Investors assessing jurisdictional risk and upside across multiple regions.
  • Regulated‑market teams aligning compliance and responsible‑gaming frameworks to multi‑jurisdiction portfolios.

  • Start at the Global Overview (index) to understand regional scale, growth, and strategic priorities.[file:1]
  • Use Regional Playbooks (Americas, Europe, Asia, Africa) to understand entry difficulty, timing, and structural themes.[file:1]

Limitations & Responsibilities

This guide is a strategic decision aid, not a legal opinion or tax advisory. It simplifies complex legal and regulatory realities into standardised labels and bands to support portfolio‑level decisions.[file:1]

  • Always obtain country‑specific legal and tax advice before committing capital or signing binding contracts.
  • Use the published ranges as directional input to your own financial models, not as standalone forecasts.
  • Expect regulatory change: tender calendars, tax rates, and licence structures may shift during 2025–2026.
