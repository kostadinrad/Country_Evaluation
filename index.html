  • Payroll social contributions: Standard rates for employees[cite: 66]
  • VAT: May not apply to gambling margin (industry-standard exemption)[cite: 67]

Justification by NAPP[cite: 68]

NAPP explicitly stated: "The decree's implementation will establish a reliable foundation for legally regulating the implementation of activities for the organization of online risk games, betting activities, and lotteries... and provide additional sources of income to the country's state budget, which can be directed to various social purposes."[cite: 68]

Revenue Capture Rationale[cite: 69]

Prior unregulated outflows: Official estimates $300M–$1B annually; unofficial estimates up to $8B.[cite: 69] The low gambling tax (4%) is designed to encourage migration from grey markets to legal operators.[cite: 70]

2.1 Average Revenue Per User (ARPU): $1,500/Month[cite: 94]

Official Source: Global Gaming Authority (GGA) / Asian Gaming Consortium[cite: 95]

Definition: ARPU is the total monthly stakes (handle) placed by an average active player, NOT the revenue retained by the operator.[cite: 96]

Derivation for Uzbekistan Context:[cite: 97]

  • Target demographic: Males aged 21–40, urban-based, football-centric[cite: 98]
  • Primary product: International football (Premier League, LaLiga, Champions League), tennis, basketball[cite: 99]
  • Typical bet size: $10–50 per wager[cite: 100]
  • Betting frequency: 30–150 bets per active player per month[cite: 101]
  • Monthly total stakes per player: $1,500[cite: 102]

Example Player Profile:[cite: 103]

  • Bets on 5–10 matches per week[cite: 104]
  • Average 3 bets per match (over/under, 1X2, accumulators)[cite: 105]
  • Stake size: $20–30 per bet[cite: 106]
  • Weekly handle: $300–400[cite: 107]
  • Monthly handle: $1,500[cite: 108]

Validation: This aligns with industry benchmarks for post-legalization markets in Central Asia/Eastern Europe where sports betting is primary product (not casino).[cite: 109]

2.2 GGR Margin: 8%[cite: 110]

Source: Standard sports betting industry margin[cite: 111]

Definition: GGR (Gross Gaming Revenue) = Stakes × Margin[cite: 112]

Calculation:[cite: 113]

  • Player stakes (handle): $1,500/month[cite: 114]
  • Bookmaker margin (hold): 8%[cite: 115]
  • Monthly GGR per player: $1,500 × 0.08 = $120[cite: 116]
  • Annual GGR per player: $120 × 12 = $1,440[cite: 117]

Why 8%?[cite: 118]

  • Sports betting has lower margins than casino (which runs 15–25%)[cite: 119]
  • Margin depends on odds accuracy, promotional burndown, and betting volume[cite: 120]
  • 8% is conservative for a well-matched market without major outlier losses[cite: 121]
  • In-play (live) betting typically generates higher margins (9–12%) due to reduced liquidity[cite: 122]

Industry Context: Major European operators target 8–10% GGR margins; 6–7% suggests high promotional activity or player-favourable odds;[cite: 123] >12% suggests potential market inefficiency or reduced competition.[cite: 124]

2.3 Effective Tax Rate: 5% (4% GGR + 1% Fund)[cite: 125]

Source: Uzbek Decree effective January 1, 2025[cite: 126]

Tax Calculation:[cite: 127]

  • Gambling Tax: 4% of GGR (mandated 2025–2030)[cite: 128]
  • Special Fund Contribution: 1% of GGR (state-designated fund for problem gambling/research)[cite: 129]
  • Total Effective Tax: 5% on GGR[cite: 130]

Example (Monthly):[cite: 131]

  • Monthly GGR (from $1,500 stakes): $120[cite: 132]
  • Gambling tax (4%): $4.80[cite: 133]
  • Fund contribution (1%): $1.20[cite: 134]
  • Total tax/fees: $6.00 (5% of GGR)[cite: 135]

Player Winnings Tax: 0% — Players do not pay personal income tax on lottery/betting winnings (incentive to use licensed platforms vs. grey market).[cite: 136]

Why Low Tax Rate? NAPP explicitly stated: "The utilization of distributed ledger technology will ensure the integrity of player, bet, and winnings databases... The low tax rate encourages migration from unregulated platforms."[cite: 137]
