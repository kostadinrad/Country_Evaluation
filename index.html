EXECUTIVE SUMMARY - WEEK OF NOV 24-27, 2025

Key Market Changes This Week

South Africa - Major Tax Reform Proposal

  • National Treasury released draft proposal: 20% national tax on online gambling.
  • Proposed to add to existing provincial taxes (6-20%), creating total 26-29% effective rate.
  • Signals potential legalization path for online casinos (currently banned under National Gambling Act 2004).
  • Market Context: Broader gambling market projected to generate EUR 3.63B by 2025, with betting accounting for 60.5% of total earnings. Mobile wagering dominates at 81% of online betting turnover.
  • Competitive Impact: Current operators (Supabets, major international brands) may face margin compression if tax passes. Regulatory separation between betting and casino platforms now strictly enforced.

Nigeria - Constitutional Crisis Escalating

  • Supreme Court ruled gaming is state-exclusive competency (Nov 22, 2024).
  • National Assembly now considering "Central Gaming Bill 2025" - directly contradicts court ruling.
  • Constitutional Context: Federal excise duty (5%) introduced Jan 2025; federal withholding tax (5%) on player wins now effective.
  • Competitive Context: BetKing saw a 76% YoY revenue surge to $106M (FY25). Bet9ja holds approximately 6% market share, and SportyBet is the 4th most-visited website nationally (July 2025).
  • Status: High regulatory uncertainty; expect 6-12 month delay in clarity.

Brazil - Market Stabilization Underway

  • Tax Collection Milestone: BRL 3.026 billion collected in taxes between January and May 2025 (23,000% increase YoY).
  • Revenue Performance: Estimated BRL 25.2 billion gross revenue in first five months of 2025, far exceeding pre-regulation projections of BRL 8-20 billion annually.
  • Market Structure: 14 definitive licenses (full authorization) + 52 provisional licenses as of Dec 2025.
  • Operator Count: 65 operators running 171 brands (August 2025 data).
  • Ordinance Implementation: Ordinance No. 827 closed regulatory loopholes as of January 2025; multi-brand operation permitted (up to 3 brands per license).
  • Marketing Requirement: Celebrity partnerships and creative promotions essential. 95% mobile adoption projected by 2030.
  • Advertising Changes: Senate approved Bill No. 2,985/2023, establishing new rules prohibiting use of artists, athletes, influencers, and public figures in betting advertisements (effective 2025).

Peru - Market Growth Accelerating

  • Market Scale: Total gambling market estimated at USD 2.51 billion (2024), with online gambling contributing approximately USD 1.2 billion.
  • 2025-2026 Projection: Online gambling sector could generate USD 145 million in revenue for 2025, with total market growth to USD 3 billion by 2026.
  • Digital Penetration: Internet penetration at 75% (early 2024) with mobile connections exceeding total population. Mobile payments account for 59% of all e-commerce transactions.
  • Regulatory Momentum: 59 operators received authorization during first year of Law No. 31557 regime (February 2024 onwards).

CRITICAL DEVELOPMENTS - SOUTH AMERICA

BRAZIL - Market Dominance & Regulatory Maturation

  • Market Scale: Largest in Latin America. Total South America market projected at USD 8.3-9.2B for 2025.
  • Revenue Trajectory: USD 1.506 billion (2024) growing to USD 3.01 billion by 2030 at 12.3% CAGR.
  • Tax Performance: May 2025 alone collected BRL 814 million in monthly taxes.
  • Key Competitors: SuperBet, MGM, Bet365, Betsson, Caesars among the 14 definitive licensees.
  • Marketing Strategy: Celebrity marketing essential as Brazilian players are responsive to influencer engagement (noting new advertisement restrictions on public figures).
  • Payment Restrictions: Credit card and cryptocurrency betting are prohibited.

Brazil - Detailed Regulatory & Market Analysis (Tier 1 Priority)

Category Expanded Regulatory & Market Detail
Regulatory Status Market officially became fully regulated on January 1, 2025, under Law No. 14,790/2023. Overseen by the Secretariat of Prizes and Bets (SPA) within the Ministry of Finance.
Licensing & Fees 14 definitive licenses granted with 52 provisional licenses pending full system certification. Concession fee: BRL 30 million (approximately USD 6 million) for up to three commercial brands. License term is five years, non-transferable.
Tax Structure Effective tax rate on Gross Gambling Revenue (GGR): 12%. Allocated to social programs: Education (10%) and Sports (36%).
Compliance Mandates Operators must establish local company with minimum 20% Brazilian shareholder. Stringent KYC/AML requiring facial recognition. All licensed operators must adopt .bet.br domain extension. Player withdrawals must process within 120 minutes.
Competitive Context Market growing at 16.51% CAGR. Sports betting segment generated 56.3% of revenue share in 2024. Marketing heavily focused on celebrity partnerships and mass-market sports sponsorship (with restrictions on athlete/influencer endorsements per Bill 2,985/2023).

Peru - Detailed Regulatory & Market Analysis (Tier 1/Urgent Entry)

Category Expanded Regulatory & Market Detail
Regulatory Status Online gaming and sports betting fully regulated under Law No. 31557, effective February 9, 2024. Regulatory body: MINCETUR (Ministry of Foreign Trade and Tourism).
Digital Infrastructure Internet penetration at 75% (early 2024). Mobile connections exceed total population. Mobile payments account for 59% of all e-commerce transactions, signaling strong mobile-first betting demand.
Licensing & Fees 59 operators authorized in first year. Capital guarantee required: approximately EUR 792,000 per license (approximately USD 235K+). Licenses granted for six years.

Compliance Mandates Servers, data, and all player information must be hosted locally in Peru. Cryptocurrency for payments explicitly prohibited. Operators must implement AML system (SPLAFT) and appoint qualified compliance officer.
Market Context Market projection for 2026: USD 850M-1.1B. December 2025 marked as URGENT for filing applications to ensure licensing completion by January 2026.

CRITICAL DEVELOPMENTS - CENTRAL AMERICA

PANAMA & COSTA RICA - Regional B2B Offshore Hubs

Panama Hub Analysis

  • License Structure: Master licenses with 7-year validity, USD 40,000 fee.
  • Tax Structure: 10% tax on GGR; essentially tax-free for income from foreign wagers.
  • Multi-Brand Authorization: Emphasized competitive advantage for multi-brand operations.
  • B2B Hub Role: Primary jurisdiction for Latin American gambling operations infrastructure.
  • Strategic Advantage: Rapid licensing, favorable tax treatment for international operations.

Costa Rica Hub Analysis

  • Licensing Approach: Operators obtain "data processing license" (non-gambling-specific designation).
  • Licensing Speed: Rapid licensing (5 business days).
  • Regulatory Efficiency: Known for operational flexibility and streamlined compliance.
  • Regional Role: Secondary B2B hub for Latin American operations.

DOMINICAN REPUBLIC - Transitional Competition & Closing Window

  • Regulatory Framework: Resolution 136-2024 (first comprehensive online framework).
  • Licensing Details: License cost USD 346,000; 5-year validity; Non-transferable for the first 3 years.
  • Competitive Landscape: Competitive window closing Q1 2026.
  • Entry Timeline: Applications should be filed immediately to secure early-mover positioning.
  • Market Potential: Growing tourism industry supports local player base expansion.

MEXICO - High-Risk, Offshore Dominance

South Africa - Detailed Regulatory & Market Analysis (Tier 2/Major Regulatory Shift)

Category Expanded Regulatory & Market Detail
Regulatory Status Online sports betting is legal and provincially regulated. Online casino/interactive gambling remains technically unlawful under National Gambling Act 2004.
Tax Reform Proposal (New) National Treasury released draft discussion paper November 25, 2025. Proposal introduces 20% national tax on Gross Gambling Revenue (GGR) for all online gambling.
Effective Tax Rate If enacted, 20% national tax would be levied in addition to existing provincial taxes (typically 6-9%), pushing total effective GGR tax rate to 26-29%.
Timeline & Impact Deadline for public comments on draft proposal: January 30, 2026. Proposed tax, while controversial, signals potential path for online casino legalization in medium term.
Competitive Context Overall market projected EUR 3.63B by 2025. Mobile wagering dominates at 81% of online betting turnover. Current operators (Supabets, major international brands) may face margin compression if tax passes.

NIGERIA - Constitutional Crisis & Federated Licensing Model

  • Crisis Status: Supreme Court vs. National Assembly standoff unresolved. 6-12 months likely regulatory vacuum.
  • Court Ruling: Supreme Court ruled November 22, 2024, that gaming is state-exclusive competency.
  • Legislative Challenge: National Assembly considering "Central Gaming Bill 2025," directly contradicting court ruling.
  • Tax Context: Federal excise duty (5%) and federal withholding tax (5%) on player wins now effective.
  • Federated Model: 28-state licensing model aligns approvals between National Lottery Regulatory Commission and state regulators (e.g., Lagos State Lotteries Board).
  • Market Leaders: BetKing saw 76% YoY revenue surge to USD 106M (FY25). Bet9ja holds approximately 6% market share. SportyBet is 4th most-visited website nationally (July 2025).
  • Entry Strategy: Three distinct pathways emerging: state-by-state, unified remote operator, and offshore routes.

Nigeria - Detailed Regulatory & Market Analysis (Tier 2/Constitutional Crisis)

Category Expanded Regulatory & Market Detail
Regulatory Status Environment in state of high uncertainty and constitutional crisis. Supreme Court ruled November 22, 2024, that gaming and lottery are Residual Matters, giving State Governments exclusive legislative control.
Federal-State Impasse National Assembly considering "Central Gaming Bill 2025," move that directly defies Supreme Court's ruling. Lagos State initiated legal steps for contempt proceedings against National Assembly.
Federated Licensing Model 28-state licensing model with federal coordination reducing duplication and strengthening AML oversight. Aligns approvals between NLRC and state regulators like Lagos State Lotteries Board.
Tax & Financials Market fragmented with federal taxes (5% excise duty and 5% withholding tax) still in place, but legality/enforcement questioned due to state-level control ruling. Market driven by high demand.
Outlook & Action Regulatory confidence is LOW, with projected 6-12 month delay in clarity. Market entry recommended to be deferred until Q2 2026, pending resolution of constitutional conflict.

KENYA - Regulatory Transition & Real-Time Monitoring

  • Regulatory Framework: Gambling Act 2025 passed; dissolved old Betting Control and Licensing Board (BCLB), creating Gambling Regulatory Authority (GRA).
  • Tax Structure Overhaul: New 5% withdrawal tax replaces prior 20% tax on net earnings + 15% excise duty.
  • Technical Requirements: Mandatory biometric player identification (fingerprints, facial/iris scans) for all new accounts. Real-time monitoring system linked to Kenya Revenue Authority enforced.
  • Local Ownership: 30% local ownership requirement adds complexity to foreign operator entry.
  • Player Base: Over 7 million Kenyans have registered sportsbook accounts (highest engagement in Africa). Football betting dominates.
  • Consolidation Outlook: Expected consolidation from 99 operators to 15-20 core survivors.

UGANDA - Rising Star with Mobile Betting Focus

  • Market Drivers: Population of 48 million embracing mobile betting via affordable smartphones and increasing internet access.
  • Betting Preference: Football remains primary focus with European leagues and local competitions attracting play.
  • Operator Growth: Government doubled number of registered betting operators, signaling strong industry support.
  • Regulatory Challenges: Concerns over gambling addiction leading to discussions about stricter regulations.
  • Competitive Advantage: Low-cost mobile betting ecosystem continues attracting both bettors and operators.

ANGOLA - New Law Stabilizing Market with Tax Reduction

  • Regulatory Framework: Law 17/24 implementation progressing.
  • Tax Structure Reform: Sports betting GGR tax set to 20% (major reduction from prior 45%).
  • License Cost: Estimates indicate approximately EUR 120K cost.
  • Entry Status: First-Mover Advantage in public tender.
  • Regulatory Clarity: Law 17/24 provides stable framework for operator planning.

GHANA - Maturing Market & Cryptocurrency Adoption

  • Market Integration: Biometric integration mandated (September 30 deadline).
  • Capital Requirements: Estimated USD 2.0M+ requirement for new operators.
  • Cryptocurrency Trend: Growing trend of cryptocurrency gambling (Bitcoin, Ethereum) for deposits/withdrawals.
  • Market Structure: Seven land-based casinos (five in Accra, rest in Kumasi and Tema). Clear regulations attract innovative operators.
  • Entry Status: Avoid (mature, saturated, consolidation phase).

AFRICA REGIONAL OUTLOOK - Growth Trajectory & Opportunities

  • Continental Growth: Africa's online gambling market expected to reach USD 11.27 billion by 2032.
  • CAGR: Projected 5.81% CAGR until 2029 across major markets.
  • Key Trends: Cryptocurrency betting will bypass banking restrictions. Mobile-first betting dominance continues as smartphone penetration increases. Regulatory improvements legalize and regulate gambling. International operators show increasing interest in Africa as high-growth frontier.
  • Responsible Gambling: AGRA guidelines require deposit limits, time-out features, self-exclusion tools. Nigeria's NLRC partnering with NGOs targeting 20% reduction in problem gambling by 2026.
