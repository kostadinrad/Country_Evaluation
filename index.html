South Africa - National Tax Reform Proposal (Public Comment Deadline: January 30, 2026)

Proposal: National Treasury released draft discussion paper November 25, 2025, proposing 20% national tax on online gambling Gross Gambling Revenue (GGR).

Effective Tax Rate Impact: If enacted, 20% national tax would stack on top of existing provincial taxes (typically 6-9%), creating total effective GGR tax rate of 26-29%.

Market Context: South African gambling market projected EUR 3.63B by 2025, with betting accounting for 60.5% of total earnings. Mobile wagering dominates at 81% of online betting turnover.

Key Details: Current provincial tax rates are 6-9% for betting and 10-15% for casinos. For example, if betting operator generates GGR of R10 million, new combined tax burden could reach 26-29% of that revenue.

Industry Reaction: Sun International CEO reports Sunbet (online platform) would face additional R275 million in tax annually if proposal had been in effect. Operators warning this would be among highest-taxed gambling jurisdictions globally and destabilize legal gaming.

Potential Upside: Proposal signals potential legalization path for online casinos (currently banned under National Gambling Act 2004), though at substantial cost.

Nigeria - Constitutional Crisis Escalates (Status: HIGH UNCERTAINTY)

Court nullified National Lottery Act 2005 and issued perpetual injunctions against Federal Government interference.

Legislative Counter-Move (December 2, 2025): Nigerian Senate passed Central Gaming Bill 2025 (HB 262) on third reading, directly contradicting Supreme Court ruling. Proposed bill aims to establish Central Gaming Commission with federal regulatory authority across all states.

Defiance Implications: Lagos State Attorney-General warned Senate passage amounts to legislative overreach and potential constitutional crisis. Legal experts argue passage violates Section 287(1) of Nigerian Constitution, which makes Supreme Court judgments binding on all authorities.

Entry Timeline: Regulatory confidence is LOW. Market entry should be deferred until Q2 2026, pending resolution of constitutional conflict. Expect 6-12 month regulatory vacuum.

Key Market Drivers & Growth Factors

Positive Growth Drivers

  • Regulatory Legalization: Brazil, Peru, Colombia, Angola all have established legal frameworks driving operator growth
  • Mobile Penetration: 75%+ internet penetration in key markets (Peru, Brazil); mobile payments now 59%+ of e-commerce
  • Cryptocurrency Adoption: Ghana, Uganda, Kenya showing cryptocurrency betting growth bypassing banking restrictions
  • Youth Demographics: African markets with 48M+ youth population (Uganda) driving long-term growth
  • Operator Consolidation: Reducing fragmentation and attracting institutional capital (e.g., MGM, Caesars, Betsson entering regulated markets)

Headwinds & Risk Factors

  • Tax Rate Increases: Brazil 18% GGR (Oct 2025), South Africa 20% proposed national tax, Colombia 19% VAT compressing margins
  • Regulatory Uncertainty: Nigeria constitutional crisis, Kenya equipment costs from biometric mandates, Uganda addiction concerns
  • Platform Restrictions: South Africa platform separation mandate, Peru cryptocurrency ban, Brazil credit card prohibition limiting revenue channels
  • Competitive Saturation: Colombia consolidating to 16-18 players, Ghana maturing with 7 casinos, Kenya consolidating from 99 to 15-20 operators
  • Responsible Gambling Mandates: Kenya real-time monitoring, Uganda addiction initiatives, AGRA guidelines increasing compliance costs
