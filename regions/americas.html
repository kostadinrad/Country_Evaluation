Americas Regional Playbook

The Americas portfolio combines B2B licensing hubs and high‑growth B2C markets across Latin America and select regional hubs. The region is flagged as “Urgent” due to time‑bound tenders, regulatory windows, and consolidation phases expected around 2025–2026.

The Americas block covers 13 markets with an estimated 2026 size of USD 18.2B–21.35B and a growth profile of 10–18% CAGR. The core focus is Latin America, with complementary B2B hubs that enable regional licensing, hosting, and structuring.

3. Country Strategy Cards

The following cards provide a concise market entry view for each Tier‑1 Latin American market, linking regional strategy to country‑level execution. Dedicated deep‑dive pages can be added under /markets/ as needed.

Panama

Regional B2B hub with a 2026 market size of USD 400M–600M and a stable regulatory framework supporting cross‑border structures.

Role: B2B licensing hub Stage: Growth / consolidation Confidence: High
  • Use Panamanian structures to serve multiple LatAm markets under a regional model.
  • Focus on licensing, infrastructure, and compliant cross‑border flows rather than pure local B2C volume.
  • Attractive for groups seeking a predictable base with regional passporting benefits.

Costa Rica

Established B2B hub with a 2026 market of USD 300M–500M, historically used for hosting, service operations, and support functions.

Role: Technical & B2B service hub Stage: Growth Confidence: High
  • Suited to platform, call‑centre, and operational support models for regional operators.
  • Less about domestic B2C scale; more about cost‑effective and flexible B2B infrastructure.
  • Often paired with licensing from other LatAm regulators or European gateways.

Paraguay

New‑law growth market sized at USD 200M–350M by 2026, with early‑mover upside under an evolving regulatory framework.

Role: High‑growth frontier Stage: Early growth Confidence: High
  • Greenfield licensing scenario under recently formalised rules for online activity.
  • Regulatory details and tender mechanics should be monitored closely for timing and caps.
  • Best suited for groups prepared to engage early with some regulatory risk.

Dominican Republic

Market estimated at USD 600M–900M by 2026, characterised by a “closing window” dynamic and urgency around securing positions.

Role: Time‑sensitive B2C Stage: Growth / pre‑consolidation Confidence: Medium
  • Window for favourable licensing or partnership terms is expected to narrow around 2025–2026.
  • Execution requires speed and strong local advisory to manage political and regulatory nuance.
  • Delay risks entering later in a more mature, less favourable regulatory environment.

Peru

Large market at USD 850M–1.1B by 2026 with an “urgent entry” signal due to follow‑up licensing cycles and the formalisation of online regulation.

Role: Core LatAm anchor Stage: Growth / early consolidation Confidence: High
  • Licensing phases and renewals around 2025–2026 create a finite window for optimised entry.
  • Requires strong compliance, responsible‑gaming frameworks, and alignment with local tax rules.
  • Should be treated as a long‑term anchor market, not a short‑term opportunistic play.

Brazil

Mega‑market with USD 3.0B–3.5B in 2026, firmly in a consolidation and M&A phase rather than a pure greenfield licensing story.

Role: Regional flagship Stage: Consolidation Confidence: High
  • Scale and competitive intensity make pure new‑entry plays structurally challenging.
  • Most rational routes involve M&A or strategic partnerships with licensed incumbents.
  • Regulatory and tax evolution should be tracked continuously for changes impacting unit economics.

Colombia

Maturing market at USD 650M–800M by 2026 with established regulation, acting as a reference for LatAm online frameworks.

Role: Benchmark regulated market Stage: Mature / consolidation Confidence: High
  • Strong local incumbents and regulated frameworks favour partnership or selective M&A.
  • Useful as a model jurisdiction for understanding LatAm technical and compliance requirements.
  • Can be combined with Peru and Brazil to form a three‑pillar LatAm strategy.

4. 2026 Entry & Investment Roadmap

The Americas roadmap sequences activity across 2026 to align licensing windows, tenders, and consolidation phases with realistic execution capacity.

4.1 Quarterly Focus

Quarter Primary Focus Illustrative Markets
Q1 2026 Secure or optimise positions in B2B hubs and straightforward licensing environments to establish foundations. Panama, Costa Rica; early scoping in Paraguay and Colombia.
Q2 2026 Execute on follow‑up licensing cycles and time‑sensitive tenders/renewals where “urgent entry” applies. Peru follow‑up licensing; Dominican Republic where windows are narrowing.
Q3 2026 Consolidation‑focused M&A scanning and negotiation in large, competitive markets with proven demand. Brazil and Colombia M&A or strategic partnerships; continued build‑out in Peru.
Q4 2026 Portfolio optimisation based on realised performance, adjusting capital between high‑growth and stable anchors. Rebalance exposure between Peru/Brazil/Colombia and smaller frontier markets.

4.2 Role in Global Portfolio

  • The Americas region delivers a significant share of global growth while also providing at least one structural anchor (e.g., Brazil or Peru) in most operator portfolios.
  • Hubs like Panama and Costa Rica should be evaluated not only on local GGR, but on their ability to support cross‑border operations and tax‑efficient structures.
  • Region‑wide exposure should be capped and stress‑tested against regulatory volatility and FX risk in LatAm currencies.
