Risk, Timing & Portfolio Roles
High‑level definitions of strategic labels used across all regions, enabling quick interpretation of country cards and tables.

Strategic Priority Labels

  • 🔴 Urgent · Time‑bound windows such as tenders, renewals, or regulatory tightening that reward early positioning.
  • 🟡 Emerging · Early‑growth markets with limited incumbents where licences or tenders are still accessible at reasonable cost.
  • 🟢 Stable · Mature, regulated markets used as revenue anchors and credibility platforms, typically with clear licensing processes.
  • 🟣 Selective · Large but highly constrained markets where only narrow, highly compliant plays are viable.

Entry Difficulty Bands

  • Low–Moderate · Transparent regulation, reasonable tax (≈ <10–15%), no structural monopolies; examples include gateways like Malta and Ireland.
  • High · Heavy tax, complex licensing, or strict enforcement that raises cost of entry and compliance.
  • Very High / Impossible · State monopolies, closed licensing, or explicit online prohibitions that effectively block new foreign operators.

Confidence Levels & Updates

  • High · Recent official data (≤ 12 months), clear regulatory frameworks, multiple converging data points and consistent 2025–2026 projections.
  • Medium · Partial data, rapidly evolving regulation, and some reliance on structured estimates or ranges.
  • Low · Limited transparency and short operating history; treated as exploratory and scenario‑based only.
